What Documents Do You Need to Apply for a Free Lifeline Phone?

June 10, 2026 · GO Advertising — authorized Assurance Wireless partner

The single biggest reason free-phone applications get delayed is missing or mismatched paperwork. The good news: once you know exactly what to bring, approval is usually fast. Here's the complete document checklist for a Lifeline / Assurance Wireless application in 2026.

1. Proof of identity

You'll need to confirm who you are. A government-issued photo ID (driver's license or state ID), passport, or similar document works. The name must match the name on your other documents.

2. Proof of eligibility — pick ONE path

Path A — program participation. If you're in a qualifying program, show a current letter or statement (typically dated within the last 12 months):

  • Medicaid / Medi-Cal
  • SNAP / food stamps (CalFresh)
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • Federal Public Housing Assistance / Section 8
  • Veterans Pension or Survivors Pension

Path B — income. If you qualify by income (at or below 135% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines), show proof such as last year's tax return, three consecutive months of pay stubs, a Social Security benefits statement, or an unemployment/retirement benefit letter.

3. Proof of address

An official document showing your current address — a utility bill, lease, mortgage statement, or government letter — helps confirm where service will be provided.

Tips to avoid rejection

  • Make sure the name matches exactly across all documents.
  • Use current documents — expired letters are the #1 cause of delays.
  • Submit clear, full-page images (no cut-off corners).
  • Remember: one benefit per household.
