How to Get Free Phone Service Through Assurance Wireless (2026 Guide)

Updated June 2026 · GO Advertising — authorized Assurance Wireless partner

If you qualify for the federal Lifeline program, you can get free phone service through Assurance Wireless — free monthly data, unlimited talk, and unlimited text at $0 a month. It's available nationwide, and in California eligible customers can also get a free smartphone. GO Advertising (BGOA) is an authorized Assurance Wireless partner that makes applying simple.

What you get

Free Assurance Wireless phone service on the T-Mobile network includes:

  • Free monthly high-speed data (about 10GB in most areas, 12GB in California)
  • Unlimited talk and unlimited text
  • $0 monthly cost
  • The ability to keep your current number
  • A free smartphone for eligible California customers (bring your own compatible phone in other states)

Keep your service active by using it at least once every 30 days.

Where is it available?

Assurance Wireless free phone service is available nationwide — in every U.S. state except Hawaii, Alaska, and Puerto Rico.

Who qualifies?

You're eligible if you take part in any one of these programs:

  • Medicaid (Medi-Cal in California)
  • SNAP / food stamps (CalFresh in California)
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • Federal Public Housing Assistance / Section 8
  • Veterans Pension Benefit or Survivors Pension

You may also qualify based on your household income (at or below 135% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines). You'll need proof of program participation or proof of income. The benefit is limited to one per household.

How to apply (step by step)

  1. Confirm you qualify — check the program or income list above.
  2. Gather proof — your benefit card, an award letter, or income documentation.
  3. Apply on the official partner page — assurancewireless.com/partner/BGOA.
  4. Get connected — once approved, your free service is activated. California customers can get a free phone the same day.

Nationwide — every U.S. state except Hawaii, Alaska, and Puerto Rico.
