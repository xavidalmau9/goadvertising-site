Can You Keep Your Number with a Free Government Phone? (BYOP)

June 10, 2026 · GO Advertising — authorized Assurance Wireless partner

Worried that getting a free government phone means giving up your number or your current device? It doesn't. With Assurance Wireless and the Lifeline program, most people can keep the number they already have — and often the phone they already love.

Yes — you can keep your number

When you switch to free Assurance Wireless service, you can port (transfer) your existing phone number over so friends, family, doctors, and employers can still reach you the same way. You can also choose a brand-new number if you prefer a fresh start.

Bring Your Own Phone (BYOP)

If your current phone is compatible with the T-Mobile network, you can usually keep using it — just activate your free Lifeline service on it. That means no learning a new device and no extra cost. BYOP is one of the most popular options in 2026.

What about a free phone?

In California, eligible customers can receive a free smartphone as part of the program. Outside California, the free monthly service is the benefit, and you bring a compatible phone of your own.

What you keep, what you get

  • Keep: your phone number, and your compatible phone if you want.
  • Get: $0/month service, free monthly data, unlimited talk and text.
  • No: contract, credit check, or monthly bill.

To transfer your number, don't cancel your old service first — start the free Assurance Wireless application and request the transfer during sign-up so your number moves over smoothly.
